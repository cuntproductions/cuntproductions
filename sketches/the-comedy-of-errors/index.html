Summary

After both being separated from their twins in a shipwreck, Antipholus and his slave Dromio go to Ephesus to find them. The other set of twins lives in Ephesus, and the new arrivals cause a series of incidents of mistaken identity. At the end, the twins find each other and their parents and resolve all of the problems caused earlier.

The Comedy of Errors is one of William Shakespeare's early plays. It is his shortest and one of his most farcical comedies, with a major part of the humour coming from slapstick and mistaken identity, in addition to puns and word play.

This was our attempt to rewrite the play, with a more simplistic yet humorous approach. Neither did our audience have any particularly great experience with the English language, nor did the actors have the required experience to memorise and perform complex dialogues, thus creating the need for the script you are presented with.
